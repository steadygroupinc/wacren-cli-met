Why It Matters

A Region at the Climate Frontline — and the Data Gap That Holds It Back

West and Central Africa is among the world's most climate-vulnerable regions. Escalating floods in the Sahel, prolonged droughts across the Lake Chad Basin, sea-level rise threatening coastal cities, and erratic rainfall devastating smallholder agriculture. These are not future risks. They are today's realities, affecting hundreds of millions of people.

Climate challenges in West and Central Africa are inherently regional, yet the data, tools, and infrastructure needed to address them remain dangerously fragmented.

CLI-MET Programme Rationale

National meteorological and hydrological services across the region are often under-resourced, working with outdated equipment and limited access to Earth Observation data. Climate research institutions and universities lack the computing infrastructure to run regional climate models. Agricultural agencies advising millions of smallholder farmers have no reliable pipeline of timely, localised agro-meteorological data.

WACREN CLI-MET exists to close this gap by making high-quality, timely climate data accessible, shareable, and actionable across the region through a trusted, African-led research and education network infrastructure.

The Problem
Fragmented Earth Observation Access
National meteorological agencies lack reliable, high-speed access to EUMETCast satellite data, global reanalysis products, and real-time sensor networks, leaving critical forecasting gaps.
Siloed National Systems
Climate data remains locked in national silos with weak cross-border data-sharing protocols, preventing the regional-scale analysis that transboundary climate hazards demand.
Insufficient Compute & Skills
Without access to HPC infrastructure and climate modelling platforms, regional scientists cannot generate the climate projections and scenario analyses essential for evidence-based policy.
Data Poverty in Agriculture
Climate-dependent sectors, particularly food and water systems, lack the localised, real-time environmental data needed to protect livelihoods and guide resource management decisions.
The Opportunity

Connectivity is the multiplier that turns climate data into climate action.

WACREN's existing regional backbone is uniquely positioned to become the climate data highway that West and Central Africa urgently needs.

Programme Pillars

Five Interconnected Pillars

Each pillar of WACREN CLI-MET reinforces the others, creating a coherent system where connectivity, data, tools, science, and skills work together.

01
Regional Climate Data Infrastructure
  • Federated access to EUMETCast, Earth Observation, and national meteorological datasets
  • FAIR-aligned data services (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable)
  • Integration with WACREN-connected data centres, clouds, and HPC resources
02
Connectivity for Climate Science
  • High-speed backbone interconnecting NRENs and research institutions
  • Secure cross-border data flows for climate modelling and early-warning systems
  • Redundant, resilient Points of Presence for critical climate services
03
Field Sensors & LoRaWAN Networks
  • LoRaWAN gateways integrated into NREN and campus networks
  • WMO-compliant automatic weather stations for real-time observation
  • Low-power sensors for rainfall, temperature, air & water quality
  • Solar-powered, resilient deployments for remote locations
04
Open Science & Collaboration
  • Implementation of UNESCO Open Science principles for climate research
  • Shared platforms for data publishing, repositories, and collaboration
  • Open tools, open standards, and reproducible climate research workflows
05
Capacity Building & Skills
  • Training in climate data analysis, HPC, and open data practices
  • Hands-on LoRaWAN deployment, sensor calibration, and data stewardship
  • Support for early-career researchers and women in climate STEM
  • Design sprints linked to real policy and sector needs
Flagship Activities

Activities

Concrete, partner-supported activities that bring the CLI-MET vision to life, producing tangible outcomes for the region.

I
EUMETCast Terrestrial Data Portal Deployment & Training
Deploying and operationalising terrestrial EUMETCast receiving stations at National Meteorological and Hydrological Services, and providing training to ensure effective use of satellite-derived Earth Observation data across the WACREN region.
Satellite · Data Access
II
Open Climate & Agriculture Data Design Sprints
Collaborative design sprints co-organised with EUMETSAT, WASCAL, ICTP, RUFORUM, and AfriGEO, bringing together meteorologists, agronomists, data scientists, and policymakers to develop open, actionable climate and agricultural data products for the region.
Agriculture · Open Data
III
Regional Climate Research Connectivity & HPC Integration
Connecting national and regional climate research centres to high-performance computing infrastructure through WACREN's high-speed backbone, enabling regional climate modelling, seasonal forecasting, and shared computing resources for complex climate simulations.
HPC · Connectivity
IV
IoT & WMO-Compliant Environmental Monitoring Station Network
Deployment of LoRaWAN-enabled, WMO-compliant automatic weather and environmental monitoring stations at WACREN-connected institutions, producing continuous, ground-truth observations that feed regional climate platforms.
IoT · Weather Stations
Global & Continental Alignment

Meeting the SDGs & Africa's Climate Commitments

WACREN CLI-MET is foundational infrastructure that accelerates progress on the region's most critical global and continental obligations.

UN Sustainable Development Goals
SDGs 2 · 6 · 7 · 13 · 17
SDG 2
Zero Hunger. Real-time agro-meteorological data from LoRaWAN weather stations and EUMETCast feeds directly supports early warning of drought, flood risk, and crop stress, enabling timely interventions that protect food security.
SDG 6
Clean Water & Sanitation. Hydrological monitoring stations and cross-border data sharing enable better river basin management, groundwater monitoring, and early warning of flood events.
SDG 7
Affordable & Clean Energy. High-resolution climate and solar radiation data support renewable energy resource assessment, hydropower planning, and climate-smart energy infrastructure investment.
SDG 13
Climate Action. CLI-MET is the core infrastructure for regional climate action, enabling National Adaptation Plans, strengthening NDCs, and giving African scientists the data and tools to drive evidence-based climate policy.
SDG 17
Partnerships for the Goals. CLI-MET operationalises multi-stakeholder partnerships between universities, regional bodies, EUMETSAT, WASCAL, ICTP, RUFORUM, and AfriGEO, pooling expertise to achieve shared climate outcomes.

Programme Impact

What Success Looks Like

Measurable, meaningful outcomes for researchers, policymakers, communities, and the region's climate future.

Faster, More Reliable Climate Data Access
Researchers, meteorologists, and policymakers gain high-speed, dependable access to satellite, in-situ, and modelled climate data, cutting the time from data collection to decision-making.
Improved Climate-Smart Agriculture
Localised agro-meteorological intelligence supports better seasonal planning, pest and disease early warning, and resilient crop management, protecting the livelihoods of millions of farmers.
Stronger Disaster Preparedness
Enhanced regional early-warning infrastructure, powered by real-time sensor networks and shared climate models, reduces the death toll and economic losses from floods, droughts, and extreme weather.
An African-Led Climate Data Ecosystem
A sustainable, open, and African-owned climate data platform, reducing dependence on external systems, building lasting institutional capacity, and positioning the region as a contributor to global climate science.
